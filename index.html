Abstract

Future anticipation, as a foundational capability of intelligent perception, empowers autonomous decision-making and human intent inference. Such predictive reasoning relies on sufficient visual observations, yet single-view perception is inherently vulnerable to pervasive perspective occlusions in real-world scenarios. By contrast, dual-view observations provide a more comprehensive scene understanding through visual complementation. After eliminating view biases, the inherent complexity of interactive scenarios remains non-trivial, which further demands fine-grained hierarchical modeling of human activities. To explore how dual-view observation and hierarchical prediction granularity facilitate future anticipation, we propose HiDBench, a multi-level benchmark that aligns exocentric and egocentric data. Curated via a VLM-and-human-in-the-loop pipeline, HiDBench provides 9,000+ aligned ego-exo video pairs and defines 9 hierarchical tasks across three levels, i.e., relation, action, and video. Extensive benchmarking of 13 state-of-the-art MLLMs reveals that dual-view input enhances performance by addressing view-specific occlusions, while also challenging the reasoning ability with complex scenes. Evaluation results further reveal a pronounced capability gap, with the best MLLM reaching only 52%, 87%, and 37% of human performance at the relation, action, and video levels, respectively. These findings underscore that complex hierarchical anticipation remains a significant challenge for MLLMs.
